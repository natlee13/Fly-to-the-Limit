A Little Bit About Us...

Our Company

Fly to the Limit scenic flights is owned by husband and wife team, Olga and Bruce MacKenzie. Born in Southland, they both have commercial pilots licences and have spent 19 and 9 years respectively, flying for Middle Eastern Airlines.

At Fly to the Limit, every plane is owner operated, with safety and maintenance the highest priority. Our operators are all trained in sustainable tourism and share a love of the mountains and our peaceful, natural scenery.
We currently have four fixed wing aircraft, one helicopter and a glider.

Our Pilots

Olga MacKenzie

Olga MacKenzie

Although Olga is very busy as co-owner of Fly to the Limit, she can't resist getting in the pilot's seat and showing our visitors what makes New Zealand special.

When not in the office Olga takes our glider tours. She loves the peace and tranquility that the glider offers. Outside of flying, Olga's other passions are skiing and snowboarding.

Wendy "Dropper" Gilhallen

Wendy "Dropper" Gilhallen

Wendy previously spent 13 years as a commercial pilot in Africa, Russia and South America, during which time she survived three crashes (none her own fault she maintains).

Wendy owns a Cessna-172 Skyhawk P that is ideal for low level sightseeing, rides a Harley and is a ski instructor during the season.

Finn McCool

Finn McCool

A stunt pilot with the Red Arrows UK Airforce Stunt Team, Finn has served in combat choppers in 3 recent wars. Finn owns an Extra EA-200 for the ultimate stunt flight experience, and flies all our other fixed wing craft much more sedately when required.

And yes, that is his real name. He’s Irish and he doesn’t want to talk about it.

Bruce MacKenzie

Bruce MacKenzie

As co-owner of Fly to the Limit, Bruce likes to take a hands on approach and can still be found pilotting the chopper on our adventure tours (we think he may just be trying to avoid doing paperwork!)

Bruce has vast experience in all of our craft but these days likes to leave the aerobatics to Finn. He prefers the golf course to G-force at his age!

Hans Gerhansen

Hans Gerhansen

Hans first took the controls of a small plane at age 12 and flew solo at 14. He later had a series of test pilot jobs, but left that as he decided he prefers company when in the air.

Hans doesn’t own his own plane right now, but you will find him in the pilot’s seat of any of our aircraft when he’s not skiing, tramping, climbing, hang-gliding or performing with his back-up band.

John "Doc" Holliday

John "Doc" Holliday

With over 10,000 hours piloting helicopters in the bush and mountains of the Southern Alps for deer recovery and rescue operations, Doc is well qualified to land you and your friends in remote parts of the country that only he knows about.

He’ll help you plot your route, drop extra provisions where you want them and he'll pick you up when you’re done.
